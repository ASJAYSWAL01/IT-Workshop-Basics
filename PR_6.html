About Vadodara

Vadodara, formerly known as Baroda, is a major city in the Indian state of Gujarat. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, beautiful architecture, and royal history. The city lies on the banks of the Vishwamitri River and is home to several educational institutions and industries. Vadodara is also famous for its vibrant Navratri celebrations.

Laxmi Vilas Palace

The Laxmi Vilas Palace, built by Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III in 1890, is one of the grandest structures in India. It is four times the size of Buckingham Palace and is known for its Indo-Saracenic architecture. The palace is still the residence of the royal family of Vadodara and houses a remarkable collection of artifacts, paintings, and weapons. Its lush gardens and opulent interiors attract tourists from across the country.
